# Wise Vocabulary Guidelines

## Product & Feature Names

### Account
- **Personal accounts:** Always lowercase "account"
- **Business accounts:** "Wise Business account" with capitals
- **Usage rule:** Only use these terms after customer verification is complete

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Your account balance"
- ✓ "Open a Wise Business account"
- ✗ "Your Account" (personal)

### Assets
- **Always capitalize:** Assets, Stocks, Interest
- **Context:** Feature that allows customers to choose how they hold money

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Explore Assets"
- ✓ "Earn with Interest"
- ✗ "invest in assets"

### Auto Conversions
- **Capitalize when referencing the feature:** "You can now use Auto Conversions"
- **Lowercase in context:** "set up an auto conversion"
- **Preferred phrasing:** "when your desired rate is met"
- **Avoid:** "hit" or "reached" when talking about rates

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Auto Conversions let you convert money automatically"
- ✓ "Your auto conversion is active"
- ✗ "when your rate hits the target"

### Balance
- **Definition:** The amount of money someone has
- **Don't use as currency identifier**

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Your available balance"
- ✓ "Add money to your GBP currency"
- ✗ "Your GBP balance" (use "GBP currency" instead)

### Groups & Jars
- **Lowercase** unless referring to the feature itself
- **Feature reference:** Capitalize when talking about the feature conceptually

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Create a new group"
- ✓ "Read more about Groups"
- ✓ "Move money to your taxes jar"
- ✗ "create a new Group" (mid-sentence)

### Main Account
- **Capitalize** at sentence start or as a label
- **Lowercase** within sentences

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Main account"
- ✓ "Move money from your main account"
- ✗ "from your Main Account"

### Wise Debit Card
- **Use full title** on first mention
- **Can shorten** to "your card" or "Wise card" after context is established

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Order your Wise debit card"
- ✓ "Freeze your card" (after card already mentioned)
- ✗ "Get a card" (first mention)

## Currency & Money

### Currency
- Use to identify specific money types in accounts
- Preferred over "balance" as identifier

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Add a currency"
- ✓ "Your available currencies"
- ✓ "Hold money in 40+ currencies"

## Banking Terminology

### Avoid Banking Terms
- **Don't use:** "bank," "banking," or "bank account" in product copy
- **Context:** Wise is not a bank
- **Exception:** Can reference "bank details" when money is being sent

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Your Wise account"
- ✓ "Add money from your bank" (when transferring in)
- ✓ "We'll need your bank details"
- ✗ "Your Wise bank account"
- ✗ "Wise banking services"

## Terms to Avoid

### Worldwide
- **Don't use:** "worldwide"
- **Use instead:** "universal" or "global"

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Send money globally"
- ✓ "Universal access"
- ✗ "Available worldwide"

### Best Rate
- **Don't claim:** "best rate"
- **Reason:** Can't guarantee superiority
- **Use instead:** "real exchange rate," "mid-market rate"

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Get the real exchange rate"
- ✓ "Use the mid-market rate"
- ✗ "We offer the best rates"

### Chat with an Agent
- **Don't use:** "agent"
- **Use instead:** "Chat with us" or "Talk with our team"

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Chat with us"
- ✓ "Talk with our team"
- ✓ "Get help from support"
- ✗ "Chat with an agent"
- ✗ "Speak to one of our agents"

## General Principles

- Be specific about what Wise is and isn't
- Avoid banking terminology that could mislead
- Use terms consistently across the product
- Don't overclaim or make guarantees we can't keep
- Keep it human — avoid corporate or agent-focused language
