# Wise Design - Tone of Voice Guide

## Overview

Wise uses a unified voice across all communications—speed, ease, and transparency are core to messaging. The voice adapts in tone depending on context, but remains distinctly Wise regardless of writer or channel.

## Three Core Principles

### Intrepid
The brand forges its own path. Being outspoken and different reflects genuine belief in a better approach, not disruption for its own sake.

Going where others won't is in our DNA. We use our voice when it's right, not just when it's convenient. We're disruptive because we believe in a different way of doing things.

### Universally Authentic
Staying true to the mission while showing up as themselves. Trust and impact come from honest, open communication across all audiences and contexts.

Authenticity means staying true to our mission and showing up as ourselves wherever we are, whoever we're speaking to, and whatever we're saying. We do it openly and honestly.

### Delightfully Simple
Simplifying international money movement—a complex process—so it feels magical and second nature rather than confusing.

We exist to simplify people's international lives. Moving money at lightning speed is complex, but it's our job to make it sound, feel, and be simple—even magical.

## How to Speak Wise

### Celebrate the Mission
Emphasize speed, ease, and transparency whenever you can. Don't be shy when talking about MONEY. Kuna to krona. Yes to yen. Win with won. But no west-centered supremacy on GBP, USD and euros please.

### Be Concise
Aim for 100% clarity in minimal words. Headlines should be so tight that subcopy feels unnecessary. Write headlines as if it's illegal to write subcopy. Universal simplicity, max transparency.

### Make It Modern
We're inclusive. For everyone, everywhere. Pick the right pronouns. Use everyday words—the ones used by everyday people. It's ok to bend grammar rules (but don't break them). Be careful with slang. LOL works on Insta, but not for investing.

### Give It Energy
Wise is going places. Just like our customers' money. Our voice is pacey, playful, and full of infectious enthusiasm to match. Paint pictures with words to show movement. F1 speeds, car sharing fees. Cairo to Cali. Here, there, everywhere.

### Add Colour
Add delight right when it's needed, not only when it's expected. It could be a little alliteration, a well-chosen cultural reference, or 1 perfect word instead of 5. Cha-ching. Each makes the experience richer. In as few words as possible, naturally.

## Right Place, Right Tone

### Standing Out
**Context:** Billboards, social ads, attention-grabbing moments

**Characteristics:**
- Short and sweet
- Full of energy
- Lots of colour

**Example:**
- ✗ "Use money like a local anywhere in the world"
- ✓ "Go global, spend local"

### Converting Customers
**Context:** Landing pages, App Store, conversion-focused channels

**Characteristics:**
- Direct and concise
- Real language, no stiff sales jargon
- Lead with the customer benefit and what people gain

**Example:**
- ✗ "The Wise debit card means you can spend abroad at the real exchange rate. No markups, no sneaky transaction fees."
- ✓ "Spend at the real exchange rate wherever you are in the world."

### Adding Delight
**Context:** Small moments, unexpected touches, everyday micro-interactions

**Characteristics:**
- Play with the unexpected
- Add a touch of humour when it suits
- Write like a 21st century human

**Example:**
- ✗ "We need to verify your identity"
- ✓ "Grab your ID"

### Providing Reassurance
**Context:** Errors, delays, problems, account issues

**Characteristics:**
- Personable and human, but to the point
- Reflect the mission pillars
- No messing with metaphors or humour

**Example:**
- ✗ "Your transfer is taking longer than expected. It will take a few more hours to complete."
- ✓ "Sorry, your transfer should have been instant. But it's going to take a bit longer this time. We'll email you as soon as it's done."

## Go Global, Write Local

Localization means transcreation—adapting concepts meaningfully for different languages, not just translating.

### Key Considerations

- **Wordplay and puns** may not translate well or be understood by customers with lower literacy levels. Ensure they work in the target language.
- **Be culturally sensitive.** Don't stereotype or make assumptions.
- **Account for text expansion.** When you translate from English, the text increases 30% on average. Sometimes it doubles. So keep it tight.
