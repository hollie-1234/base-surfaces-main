 # Writing Guidelines
Look at your content and challenge yourself to:
- Make it scannable. Most people have limited attention and skim the content.
- Use less content. Fewer words means a lighter cognitive load.
- Keep it simple. A complicated page makes the product look complicated and intimidating. Simple equals easy.

Focus on what’s useful. Customers read only what’s important to them. Guide with structure. Help customers find your message by using hierarchy, bullet points, and visual space.Make sure every word has a clear purpose and provides value to the customer. If it doesn’t—cut it. Be brutal! Only add back words if you start losing the meaning.

## Wise's Brand Principles
The foundations that guide our voice. The why behind our words. The rails that keep us on track, and always looking forwards.

### Intrepid
Forget the road less travelled. We forged our own. Going where others won’t is in our DNA. As is using our voice. Being outspoken when it’s right, not just when it’s convenient. But we’re not different to be disruptive. We’re disruptive because we believe in a different way of doing things.

### Universally authentic
Authenticity means staying true to our mission. And showing up as ourselves. Wherever we are, whoever we’re speaking to and whatever we’re saying. We do it openly and honestly. It’s how we create trust — and genuine impact.

###  Delightfully simple
We exist to simplify people’s international lives. To put the power, pounds and pesos back into their hands. Moving money at lightning speed is complex. But it’s our job to make it sound, feel and be simple. Even magical. So it’s more second nature, less second guessing.

Be mindful of wordplay and puns as these may not translate well or be understood by customers with lower literacy levels.

Be culturally sensitive. Don't stereotype or make assumptions.

Remember when you translate from English, the text increases 30% on average. Sometimes it doubles. So keep it tight.

## How to Speak Wise

### Celebrate the mission
Highlight speed, ease and transparency whenever you can. Don't be shy when talking about MONEY. Kuna to krona. Yes to yen. Win with won. But no west-centred supremacy on GBP, USD and euros please.

### Be concise
Aim for 100% clarity, as briefly as possible. Words must inspire confidence, not cause confusion. Write headlines as if it's illegal to write subcopy. Universal simplicity, max transparency.

### Make it modern
We're inclusive. For everyone, everywhere. Pick the right pronouns. Use everyday words — the ones used by everyday people. It's ok to bend grammar rules (but don't break them). Be careful with slang. LOL works on Insta, but not for investing.

### Give it energy
Wise is going places. Just like our customers' money. Our voice is pacey, playful, and full of infectious enthusiasm to match. Paint pictures with words to show movement. F1 speeds, car sharing fees. Cairo to Cali. Here, there, everywhere.

### Add colour
Add delight right when it's needed, not only when it's expected. It could be a little alliteration, a well-chosen cultural reference, or 1 perfect word instead of 5. Cha-ching. Each makes the experience richer. In as few words as possible, naturally.

## Context-Specific Tone

### Standing out (billboards, social ads, attention-grabbing)
- Short and sweet
- Full of energy
- Lots of colour

**Don't:** "Use money like a local anywhere in the world"
**Do:** "Go global, spend local"

### Converting customers (landing pages, App Store)
- Direct and concise
- Real language, no stiff sales jargon
- Lead with the customer benefit and what people gain

**Don't:** "The Wise debit card means you can spend abroad at the real exchange rate. No markups, no sneaky transaction fees."
**Do:** "Spend at the real exchange rate wherever you are in the world."

### Adding delight (small moments, unexpected touches)
- Play with the unexpected
- Add a touch of humour when it suits
- Write like a 21st century human

**Don't:** "We need to verify your identity"
**Do:** "Grab your ID"

### Providing reassurance (errors, delays, problems)
- Personable and human, but to the point
- Reflect the mission pillars
- No messing with metaphors or humour

**Don't:** "Your transfer is taking longer than expected. It will take a few more hours to complete."
**Do:** "Sorry, your transfer should have been instant. But it's going to take a bit longer this time. We'll email you as soon as it's done."

## Grammar & Style

- Use British spelling when writing in English

### Voice and Structure
- **Active voice** — Make it clear who does what. "Jon broke the sauna" not "The sauna got broken by Jon"
- **Contractions** — Use them. "We're, you're, can't" feels natural and modern
- **Second person** — Address the user as "you" in product screens

### Capitalisation
- **Sentence case** — Only capitalize the first word and proper nouns. "Sending money internationally" not "Sending Money Internationally"
- **Currencies** — Capitalize the country, lowercase the currency name: "US dollar" not "US Dollar"

### Numbers and Data
- **Use numerals** — Write "10" not "ten". Exception: spell out when starting a sentence
- **Abbreviate large numbers** — 3k, 2.5m, 1bn (lowercase k, m, bn)
- **Dates** — Format as dd/mm/yyyy. No ordinals: "1 March" not "1st March"
- **Percentages** — Use % symbol: "75%" not "75 percent"
- **Currency codes** — Space after amount: "50 BRL" not "50BRL"

### Punctuation
- **Full stops** — Break up body copy for readability. Don't use in headings or subheadings
- **Em dash** — Use with spaces for emphasis: "We tried—it didn't work"
- **En dash** — Use for ranges: "1–10" not "1-10"
- **Ellipses** — No spaces: "I'm not sure...but I'll work it out"
- **Bullet points** — One point per bullet, no periods. Capitalize if intro is complete sentence
- **Oxford comma** — Only when clarity requires it
- **Exclamation marks** — Avoid. Use sparingly, never multiple

### Word Choices
- **Login/Log in** — "Login" is the noun/adjective, "log in" is the verb
- **Change vs Edit** — "Change" switches options, "Edit" modifies content
- **Choose vs Select** — "Choose" for decisions, "Select" for picking options
- **View** — Use "View" in instructions, not "See"
- **Text** — Say "text" not "SMS" or "text message"
- **wifi** — All lowercase, no hyphen
- **email** — No hyphen, lowercase

### What to Avoid
- **Ampersands** — Write "and" (exception: brand names like "Tiffany & Co.")
- **Emoji** — Not accessible or easily localized
- **Brackets** — Only for abbreviations or data. Keep punctuation outside unless entire sentence is bracketed
- **Acronyms** — Only use widely understood ones
