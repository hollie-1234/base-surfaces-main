# Critical Banner Component — Writing Guidelines

## When to Use

Only deploy critical banners when:
- User's account is blocked, closing, or at risk of either
- User has a short time frame in which to act
- Account closure or permanent blocking is a genuine risk

### When NOT to Use
- Negative situations without urgency
- Single-activity failures (like a failed transfer)
- Non-critical information
- Situations that can be communicated through other means

## Title Guidelines

### Rules
- Explain the situation and what the user needs to do
- Stay concise and direct (1-2 sentences maximum)
- Use plain, active voice language
- Apply sentence case formatting with a period at the end

### Example
"We've temporarily blocked your account because we need to verify your identity."

## Description (Optional)

### When to Include
Only when you need to give the user a time frame in which to act.

### Rules
- Contain only one sentence in plain language
- Use sentence case with a period
- Be specific about deadlines

### Example
"You need to verify your identity by 15 March 2026 to keep your money moving."

## Button Copy

### Rules
- Start with a verb, like "Pay" or "Send"
- Limit to 1-2 words ideally
- Make the next action obvious to users
- Avoid first-person pronouns (me, my, I)
- Use sentence case without punctuation

### Examples

#### ✓ Do
- "Verify identity"
- "Unblock account"
- "Update details"

#### ✗ Don't
- "Verify my identity"
- "Click here"
- "Learn more"

## Display Rules

- Display only one banner at a time for a single message
- Never make it dismissible — keep it visible until resolved
- Consider alternative contact methods for urgent situations
- On mobile, account for accessibility font size preferences

## Tone

Be direct and clear without being alarming. The user needs to understand:
1. What the problem is
2. Why it matters
3. What they need to do
4. When they need to do it by (if applicable)
