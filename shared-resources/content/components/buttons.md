# Button Component — Writing Guidelines

## Core Principles

Button copy should:

- **Start with a verb** — Use action words like "Pay" or "Send"
- **Be concise** — Aim for just 1-2 words
- **Describe the action** — Users should understand what happens next by reading only the button
- **Connect to context** — Mirror terminology from surrounding content and page titles
- **Avoid first-person pronouns** — Don't use "me," "my," or "I"
- **Use sentence case** — Capitalize only the first letter of the first word
- **Omit punctuation** — No periods or full stops

## Examples

### ✓ Do
- "Log in"
- "Freeze card"
- "Send"
- "Pay"

### ✗ Don't
- "I want to log in to my account" — Too wordy, uses first-person
- "Freeze my card" — Unnecessary possessive pronoun
- "Go" — Too vague
- "Submit" — Generic, doesn't describe the specific action

## Why It Matters

Keep button text brief and scannable. Longer labels are harder to process and increase cognitive load—especially problematic on small devices where verbose buttons can dominate the screen and reduce overall usability.
