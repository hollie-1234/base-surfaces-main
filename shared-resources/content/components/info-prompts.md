# Info Prompt Component — Writing Guidelines

## General Principles

All info prompts should:

- **Be clear:** Explain what's happened and what to do
- **Be concise:** Try to use no more than 1-2 short sentences
- **Include key details:** Specify dates, amounts, or names to make the message actionable
- **Use full sentences:** Write in complete sentences with full stops

## Headings

Keep headings short and clear so they support rather than compete with your main message.

## Tone by Type

### Error (Red)

#### Purpose
Tell the user something went wrong and what to do about it.

#### Guidelines
- Provide a clear next step — explain what the user needs to do to fix the problem
- For complex issues, include brief explanations of why the problem occurred
- **Avoid blame:** Don't make the user feel at fault for the error

#### Example
"We couldn't verify your payment method. Check your card details and try again."

### Warning (Amber)

#### Purpose
Alert the user to a potential problem before it happens.

#### Guidelines
- Explain the potential issue — include what could happen
- Give a clear action — tell the user what they can do to avoid the problem
- Be specific about consequences and next steps

#### Example
"Your card expires on 31 March. Add a new one to avoid failed payments."

### Success (Green)

#### Purpose
Confirm a successful action and reassure the user.

#### Guidelines
- **Reassure the user:** Above all else, confirm the action was successfully completed
- **Use a warm tone:** As if you're telling someone good news, but avoid phrases that feel casual
- **Include key details:** Like amounts, names, or dates

#### Example
"You sent 500 EUR to Maria Santos. It should arrive by 12 March."

### Neutral (Grey)

#### Purpose
Provide factual information without urgency or emotion.

#### Guidelines
- Keep tone factual and straightforward
- Prioritize the key information — avoid lengthy explanations
- Avoid adding color or urgency to your message

#### Example
"Your transfer is being processed. We'll email you when it arrives."

### Proposition (Blue)

#### Purpose
Introduce a feature or opportunity that might benefit the user.

#### Guidelines
- **Frontload the benefit:** Make it immediately clear why this might help the user
- **Match tone to context:** Friendly and informative, not pushy
- **Avoid generic language:** Avoid phrases like "Check this out"
- **Don't oversell:** If the feature is useful, the benefit should speak for itself

#### Example
"Hold money in multiple currencies to save on exchange rates."

## Links

- Only add links if users need extra information to finish their task
- Avoid using more than one link
- Make link text specific and actionable

### Examples

#### ✓ Do
- "View transfer details"
- "Update payment method"

#### ✗ Don't
- "Click here"
- "Learn more"
